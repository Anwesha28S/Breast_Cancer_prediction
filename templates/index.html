Wisconsin Breast Cancer Prediction dataset

Data Set Information:

  • The dataset includes 569 instances:

    • The Wisconsin Breast Cancer (Diagnostic) dataset contains measurements derived from fine needle aspirate (FNA) images of breast masses.
    It consists of 569 samples with 30 numerical features describing characteristics of cell nuclei, and a binary target indicating whether the tumor is benign or malignant.

Attribute Information

  • All are in arbitrary image units from FNA images
  • Mean Concave Points: (Range : 0.00 – 0.20)
      Represents the average number of concave portions (inward curves) of the cell nucleus boundary.
      Higher values indicate more irregular and indented cell shapes.
      Often associated with malignant tumors
  • Worst Concave Points:(Range : 0.00 – 0.29)
      Highlights the most irregular region of the nucleus.
  • Worst Radius: (Range: 7.93 – 36.04)
      The maximum distance from the center to the boundary of the nucleus.
      Malignant tumors generally have larger and more variable nuclei.
  • Worst Area: (Range 185.2 – 4254.0)
      Represents the largest area of the cell nucleus measured.
      Correlates with nucleus size and abnormal cell growth.
  • Worst Perimeter: (Range 50.41 – 251.2)
      Maximum perimeter length,indicates the extent and irregularity of the cell shape.
